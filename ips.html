Question 3: Chisingtan Scenic Area

Question 3 tells us to observe the Chisingtan Scenic Area and how the people around there use their environment to make a living. One of the main attractions of the Chisingtan Scenic Area was the rocky shore. On those shores, we found pieces of beautiful green rocks (although we weren't sure what kind of rocks they were). On the way back to the bus, I saw a lot of old ladies selling these beautiful pieces of jewlry on little stands along the pathway. Each necklace and ring had a shiny and polished gem, usually a green one which made me realize. These ladies are using the abundant resources of gems on the shore to create products and attract tourists. Green gems are usually associated with emeralds or jade (which will be our focus). Jade in Chinese belief are greatly thought of as a precious gem. That's why you usually see a lot of older Chinese women wearing the cool gems on their necks and wrists. Using this information, the sellers take the green gems and polish them to turn them into sellable products. Although they may not be jade stones, they could be convincing enough to look like them or attract people anyway due to the shiny glimmer of the stone. That's how these women make money from the beach!
